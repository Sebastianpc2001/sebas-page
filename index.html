I'm Sebastian Peñaherrera, a biotechnology engineer and entrepreneur focused on curing age-related diseases.

Now

I am interested in developing high-effort, high-impact science into interventions that help healthy individuals live substantially longer. My work sits at the intersection of regenerative medicine, mitochondrial biology, translational research, and precision health.

Research

My scientific work has centered on closely mimicking human physiology through in vitro 3D culture systems to test mitochondrial transfer and stem cell-derived therapies for wound healing, senescence reduction, and tissue regeneration. I designed and tested a protocol that reduced senescence markers by 36% in induced senescent fibroblasts, and I have contributed to collaborative work on skin healing, intercellular communication, extracellular mitochondrial DNA, and the therapeutic potential of long non-coding RNAs.

Scientific Background

As Director’s Research Assistant at the Biomedical Institute at USFQ, I worked across regenerative medicine, cell culture, mitochondrial research, and translational bioengineering. I also applied bioinformatics pipelines to identify molecular targets involved in skin metabolism, collaborated with Columbia University on nomenclature for cell-free mitochondrial DNA, and studied whale airway microbiota using Oxford Nanopore Sequencing and QIIME2.

Publications and Patent

I have authored peer-reviewed publications in regenerative medicine and mitochondrial science, including work on mitochondrial transfer/transplant, extracellular mtDNA, and mitochondria as living drugs. I am also co-owner of a submitted patent focused on priming MSCs to produce conditioned medium that reduces senescence and enhances fibroblast proliferation.

Building

Beyond research, I founded a biotechnology startup, secured non-dilutive funding to advance R&D and early market entry, and built teams across science, operations, and product execution. I care about translating frontier science into systems that can reach people outside the lab.

Recognition

I am a TIME Fellow alumnus, Start Fellowship alumnus, IQ Conekta alumnus, and magna cum laude graduate of Universidad San Francisco de Quito.
